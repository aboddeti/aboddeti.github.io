Anderson Localization

Anderson localization of light is an exotic mesoscopic phenomenon sustained in disordered systems through the self-interference of multiply scattered light. The localized modes are essentially eigenfunctions of the structural disorder, and define the resonances in the system. Here we compute the figures-of-merit of Anderson cavities in two-dimensional membrane based structures. In these structures the disorder is written on a periodic-on-average template. We propose a disorder parameter that better reflects the randomization of the lattice points as compared to the conventionally used percentage disorder strength. Our results investigate the viability of such cavities in applications such as random lasing and cavity quantum electrodynamics.

The seminal work by Sajeev John, Strong localization of photons in certain disordered dielectric superlattices has shown the route to achieve Anderson Localization by satisfying the Ioffe-Regel criterion. Peridic-on-average-random-ssytems (PARS) can be used for achieving strong localization.

To conclude, in this work we have shown the fundamental figure of merit that governs light-matter interaction in periodic-on-average-random-systems (PARS). The Anderson localized modes originating in such systems can potentially be used to create on-chip low threshold random lasers and also be used for achieving strong-coupling for Cavity-QED applications.
