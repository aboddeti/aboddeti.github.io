Spectral Domain Inverse Design

Inverse design techniques in the context of nanophotonics have helped in discovery of compact and counter-intuitive structures/shapes. In this work we have introduced the concept of spectral domain inverse design to search through the optical trade-space (dispersive permittivity) of nanocomposite metamaterials. We developed a novel hybrid optimization technique that combines genetic algorithms and gradient descent methods. This technique is utilized to inverse design an ultra-thin thermophotovoltaic emitter coating material. Our work can lead to an efficient approach to search for new multi-functional optical/thermal metamaterials with desired complex permittivity.

Key Idea

The key idea is to find a new nano-composite disordered metamaterial that meets the desired spectral features.

We used a hybrid optimization technique to simultaneously optimize th reflection and transmission spectra.
