2019

  1. Spectral domain inverse design for accelerating nanocomposite metamaterials discovery Boddeti, Ashwin K, Alabassi, Abubakr, Aggarwal, Vaneet, and Jacob, Zubin Optical Materials Express 2019 [Abs] [HTML]

    Inverse design techniques in the context of nanophotonics have helped in discovery of compact and counter-intuitive structures/shapes. We introduce the concept of spectral domain inverse design to search through the optical trade-space (dispersive permittivity) of nanocomposite metamaterials. We develop a hybrid optimization technique that combines genetic algorithms and gradient descent methods. We utilize this technique to inverse design an ultra-thin thermophotovoltaic emitter coating material. Our work can lead to an efficient approach to search for new multi-functional optical/thermal metamaterials with desired complex permittivity.
  2. Fundamental figure of merit for engineering dipole-dipole interactions Cortes, Cristian L, Newman, Ward, Boddeti, Ashwin K, Sentz, Tyler, and Jacob, Zubin In CLEO: QELS_Fundamental Science 2019 [Abs] [HTML]

    Over the last decade there has been a debate regarding the role of the photonic environment in enhancing, inhibiting and imparting coherence to dipole-dipole interactions. We develop a unified figure of merit to conclusively explain multiple recent experiments.

2017

  1. Figures-of-merit of Anderson localization cavities in membrane-based periodic-on-average random templates Boddeti, Ashwin K, Kumar, Randhir, and Mujumdar, Sushil Optics Communications 2017 [Abs] [HTML]

    Anderson localization of light is an exotic mesoscopic phenomenon sustained in disordered systems through the self-interference of multiply scattered light. The localized modes are essentially eigenfunctions of the structural disorder, and define the resonances in the system. In this paper, we report on the computed figures-of-merit of Anderson cavities in two-dimensional membrane based structures, in which the disorder is written on a periodic-on-average template. We propose a disorder parameter that better reflects the randomization of the lattice points as compared to the conventionally used percentage disorder strength. Our results investigate the viability of such cavities in applications such as random lasing and cavity quantum electrodynamics.

  1. A single ion coupled to a UV fiber-cavity Meyer, Hendrik-Marten, Ballance, Timothy, Boddeti, Ashwin, Kobel, Pascal, Kluge, Kilian, Reichel, Jakob, and KÃ¶hl, Michael In APS Division of Atomic, Molecular and Optical Physics Meeting Abstracts 2016 [Abs] [HTML]

    Trapped ions offer long trapping and coherence times, which is why they are of great interest for the use as stationary qubits in a quantum network. Here we investigate the integration of fiber based cavities into ion traps, which due to their small mode volume and direct coupling to a single mode fiber present an efficient interface between single ions and single photons. In the past, ions and fiber-cavities have been successfully combined in the infra-red spectral range. Since ions typically have their strongest dipole transition in the ultra-violet (UV), the extension of fiber cavities to work in the UV is necessary. We will present our latest results on trapping Ytterbium ions inside approximately 150 micro-meter long fiber-cavities, which are resonant with the S-P electric dipole transition at 370nm.

  1. Order- and disorder-induced optical microcavities in membrane based photonic crystals Boddeti, Ashwin K. 2015 [Abs] [PDF]

    Photonic crystals are composite optical materials in which the refractive index varies periodically on the length scales comparable to the wavelength of light. These periodic structures forbid the transmission of light of specific frequencies through them. This provides the opportunity to guide and mold the flow of light in such structures. Photonic crystals can be used to realize optical res- onators, which are essentially storage devices. Opitcal resonators help in strong coupling between the cavity and the atom or molecules. In this thesis, we report experimental and numerical results, on how photonic crystal structures can be used to guide light or store light in optical cavities. Further we study the possibility of achieving large modal volumes and pure resonating modes in nearly periodic photonic crystal structures which is a prerequisite for lasing. Our numerical re- sults which show that not only ordered structures can form resonators but also photonic crystal structures with low disorder incorporated in them can form optical resonators having high quality factors.
